What began as a small five-thousand word idea quickly evolved into a novella with sixty-three thousand large printed words divided among 470 pages. But hey, if you're going to give someone a story for a birthday gift, it had better be good. And in the case of Jack Stern, a grizzled detective-agent on the run with a dognapped Great Dane as his only companion, "good" might not be good enough. People are after him, and they want the dog back.

With a touch of Reacher's intensity, Pendergast's enigmatic charm, along with a sprinkle of Wolfe and a dash Christie's classic mystery-solving finesse, this novella is the perfect blend of suspense and entertainment (if you're my dad). Follow Jack Stern as he tries to figure out what to do with the massive creature in his tiny Miata.

Oh, and there's missing whiskey that needs to be found.
