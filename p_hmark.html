Hallmark, ranked.

My friends and I have an ongoing Hallmark movie video chat where we watch and discuss the movies. More often than not, we nitpick until there's little left but a pile of threads.

Next Stop Christmas (1st watched)

Bigger, better, and not bad. But why put an engagement ring into a snowball and throw it at the intended?

The Irresisitible Blueberry Farm (3rd)

This XXX movie earned a huge bump in our ranks for being a hallmark movie, but just a little bit different: It dared to put The Kiss first.

Easter Under Wraps (6th)

Despite the disorienting depth of field and same-time-of-day lighting, this was the only Hallmark movie -- of the five before it -- that compelled two friends to finish the last twenty minutes a day after their first watch-through was interrupted.

A Very Nutty Christmas

A MJH special -- Lifetime, Not Hallmark -- that was so bad it was good.

Love in Store (4th)

The story of two competing and co-working television hosts forced to be co-hosts.

Chasing Leprechauns (5th)

A confusing movie best not nitpicked lest it drops lower. Another stellar example of the Love Through Playful Wrestling / Snowfight trope. Why didn't they investigate the obvious Leprechaun dwelling?

Crashing Through The Snow (2nd)

Near unaninmously disliked in our group for being boring, the movie gets some credit for [trying] to tackle adult issues in an adult way. Not without a fun sauna Hallmark moment.
