Hallmark, ranked.

The gusty Irresisitible Blueberry Farm gets the usual third act kiss over and done with right away.

My five friends and I have an ongoing Hallmark movie video chat where we watch, discuss, and nitpick Hallmark movies until there is little left but a tangly mess of thread. From BEST to WORST:

Next Stop Christmas

Bigger, better, and not bad. But why put an engagement ring into a snowball and throw it at the intended? The movie we watched first. Watched first.

The Irresisitible Blueberry Farm

This XXX movie earned a huge bump in our ranks for being a hallmark movie, but just a little bit different: It dared to put The Kiss first. We watched The IBF as our 3rd movie.

Easter Under Wraps

Despite the disorienting depth of field and same-time-of-day lighting, this was the only Hallmark movie -- of the five before it -- that compelled two friends to finish the last twenty minutes a day after their first watch-through was interrupted. Our sixth movie to discuss.

The Nine Lives of Christmas

Superman after he was superman. A fireman with a huge house and an even bigger crush...

A Very Nutty Christmas

A MJH special -- Lifetime, Not Hallmark -- that was so bad it was good. As our 8th movie, it might have ranked lower had we watched it earlier.

Love in Store

The story of two competing and co-working television hosts forced to be co-hosts. Our fourth movie.

Chasing Leprechauns

A confusing movie best not nitpicked lest it drops lower. Another stellar example of the Love Through Playful Wrestling / Snowfight trope. Why didn't they investigate the obvious Leprechaun dwelling? Our 5th movie left a too dispropportionate question:answer ratio.

Crashing Through The Snow

Near unaninmously disliked in our group for being boring, the movie does earn credit for [trying] to tackle adult issues in an adult way as well as having one of the steamier romance scenes. For being our second movie, it nearly scuttled the ship.
